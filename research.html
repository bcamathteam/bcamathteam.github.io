Although the BCA Math Team has been typically geared towards math competitions, we have begun a new initiative to inform team members and other highschoolers about the world of mathematics research. This page will eventually serve a similar role to our "Contest Prep" page, detailing opportunities and information about math research programs and competitions.

    The BCA Math Team is running a student-led informational session on Math Research for Highschoolers! This event will take place Friday, August 25th in Commons @ BCA from 3:00-7:00pm

    Join Us to Learn student perspectives on the Following:

    • What is Math Research? What are ways math is used in research?
    • Differences in Math Research at the High School vs Graduate/Professional level
    • What are ways to get research mentors?
    • How to reach out to professors from a Yale Computer Science Researcher (and how to run a drip email campaign)
    • How to apply to competitive math research programs (PRIMES, RSI, PROMYS, etc.)
    • How to brainstorm your own topic for a research project
    • How to compete with your research
    • High School/College Research Journals/Conferences
    • Research Competitions (ISEF, Regeneron STS, ST-Yau, JSHS, etc.)
    • Tips from successful student researchers (AMS Karl Menger Award @ ISEF, RSI Scholars, etc. )

    The end goal of this session is for each student to have their own project idea and a timeline for how they could approach it over the school year. If you are interested but cannot attend, all materials from the session will be posted to the archive.
