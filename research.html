Although the BCA Math Team has been typically geared towards math competitions, we have begun a new initiative to inform team members and other highschoolers about the world of mathematics research. This page will eventually serve a similar role to our "Contest Prep" page, detailing opportunities and information about math research programs and competitions.

If you are considering speaking at or sponsoring this event and would like more details, please contact kriram24@bergen.org

The BCA Math Team is running a two-day informational session on Math Research for High School Students! This event will tentatively be held on Thursday, August 24th and Friday, August 25th. Location TBD (could be BCA Campus or nearby venue).

Join Us to Learn the Following:

  • What is Math Research? How can advanced mathematics be used to further research in other disciplines?
  • Differences in Math Research at the High School vs Graduate/Professional level
  • What are ways to get research mentors?
  • How to reach out to professors (and how to run a drip email campaign)
  • How to apply to competitive math research programs (PRIMES, RSI, PROMYS, etc.)
  • How to select your own topic for a research project
  • How to compete with your research
  • High School/College Research Journals/Conferences
  • Research Competitions (ISEF, Regeneron STS, ST-Yau, JSHS, etc.)
  • Tips from successful researchers

The end goal of this session is for each student to have their own project idea and a timeline for how they could approach it over the school year.
